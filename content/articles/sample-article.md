The Indian Ocean is no longer treated as a secondary theatre in strategic studies. It is a primary route of global trade, energy movement, and naval projection. For India, the changing military and commercial profile of this ocean requires a layered and realistic doctrine.

## Why chokepoints matter

Three spaces dominate contemporary planning:

- Strait of Hormuz
- Bab-el-Mandeb
- Strait of Malacca

Any disruption across these routes quickly affects energy security, insurance costs, and diplomatic leverage. India's maritime strategy therefore has to combine naval readiness with economic resilience.

## Policy implications for India

A credible policy posture demands synchronized investment in naval modernization, regional diplomacy, and domain awareness systems.

### Recommended priorities

- Expand maritime surveillance interoperability with friendly navies.
- Increase coast guard and logistics coordination in island territories.
- Build academic-policy pipelines for maritime scenario analysis.

In short, maritime geopolitics should be viewed not only as a defense issue but as a development and governance priority.
